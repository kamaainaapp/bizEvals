How it works
From expert template to grade
Task creation
Domain experts (Finance, Operations, Sales, HR, and other areas) describe the situation in plain language. A technical teammate turns that into a runnable test. Experts fill in:
  • The prompt / goal
  • The messy starting situation (emails, policies, data across tools)
  • The golden final state: what systems should look like if everything was done correctly
  • Key judgment points they want to test
Grading
Automatic checks (assertions) verify the final state of simulated tools (e.g. “Was this invoice logged with the exact amount?”), enabling partial credit and a strict pass/fail. Human review of reasoning focuses expert time on business taste: conflicting policies, logical choices, evidence-based decisions, not every mechanical field.
Example: daily morning ops for a small café
A solopreneur or small restaurant owner where the agent must:
  • Pull yesterday’s sales from POS and online ordering
  • Review data in Sheets alongside inventory
  • Check overnight customer reviews
  • Decide supplier reorders from trends and the coming weekend
  • Update inventory counts and reorder quantities
  • Place orders via email or portal
  • Send a team note (Slack or text) about 86’d items or promotions
Real judgment on quantities, trends, and communication, while most scoring still comes from assertions on spreadsheets and messages.
